\subsection{Definition of Group}

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Problem 1, breakable] 
    Write a careful proof that every group is the group isomorphism
        of a groupoid. 
    In particular, every group is the group automorphism of some object 
        of some category.
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{tcolorbox}[title=Problem 2, breakable] 
    Consider the `sets of numbers' listed in $\sec 1.1$ and deie 
    which are made into groups by conventional operations such as $+$ and $\cdot$.
    Even if the answer is negative, see if variations on the definitions of these sets 
    lead to groups.
\end{tcolorbox}
